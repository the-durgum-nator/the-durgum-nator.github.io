Hello, it's been a bit!
I tried managing my blog and avoiding Insta for a bit with this website.

...but eventually, time beckoned and I had to trek to India. And social media is kind of built for showcasing moments.


And also, I needed the break from worrying about employment and general stress about forwarding my life.
We have plenty of that by now, and overall, it was a more semi-pleasant experience than I expected.
From -5 <-> 5, a solid 3.

That said, I wanted to try and document my thought I've had for a while before going and during the entirity of India.

Patience is something I was taught when I was young.
But I feel like it's been sorely lost over time as I've grown up.

Like, not even personally, but as a whole societally in America we seem to be rushing children to become adults.
Rushing people into careers/paths of life without actually giving guidance on how to proceed.
Pushing people to get as rich and/or as attractive as possible without regard to them as people.

I witnessed my older cousin while I was residing my uncle's place live leisurely without being in a job, and to my surprise no-one was being on him for it. Sure, he handled tasks and chores around the house, but no more than I did when I was able to drive.
<br>(Nanu if you're reading this somehow, you're my homeslice for real)


And it took me a bit aback. Is working all I want in life? Isn't there more to being a software engineer, than being a software engineer for a company?

And I felt hollow. Here I am, withering my own life away on some grind towards a goal that was more "need it to need it."

Right now the world is hectic, and really out of normalcy. It's easy to forget that, and easy to forget it will get tougher.
But I think going back and getting some of that patience when we were young might help us in the long-haul.
